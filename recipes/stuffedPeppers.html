Stuffed Peppers

Stuffed peppers are a classic easy and filling dish I love to make as a university student who meal preps to save time! The dish is so easy to make which is vital for people who don't want to revisit the grocery store after multiple failed attempts. I tend to make the portion size which this recipe makes 6. Then, I freeze and refrigerate my portions. This is a great idea for reheating. I'd recommend not placing cheese until you're ready to reheat!

The recipe consists of hollowed peppers which are filled with a stuffing of ground beef, tomato sauce , veggies, rice, and cheese. This dish has seasonings that are similar to a taco seasoning mix. I like to add spinach to mine as well for added greens!

Ingredients

  • 1lb 90% lean ground beef
  • 1¼ teaspoons salt, divided
  • Heaping ¼tsp baking soda
  • 3 large red, yellow, or orange bell peppers, cut in half from the stem to the bottom and cored
  • 3tbs oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1½tsp chili powder
  • ½tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 (8-oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup cooked rice, quinoa, or any grain
  • 1½ cups shredded Monterey Jack or Cheddar Jack cheese (I personally just use mozzarella)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F and set an oven rack to the middle position.
  2. Tenderize beef: In a medium bowl, using your hands, mash the beef with 1tsp of salt and the baking soda. Let sit for 20 mins while you continue with the recipe.
  3. Line a baking dish with aluminium foil and place the peppers, cut side up, in the dish. drixxle 1tbsp of the oil and sprinkle the remaining salt.
  4. Roast the peppers for about 20 minutes, until slightly browned and tender-crisp. A bit of liquid will accumulate in the bottom of the peppers; that's okay.
  5. Meanwhile, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat in a large nonstick skillet. Add the onion and cook, stirring frequently, until soft and translucent, 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook 1 minute more; do not brown. Add the ground beef mixture, chili powder, cumin, and oregano and increase the heat to medium high. Cook, breaking the meat up with a wooden spoon, until the meat is browned and almost cooked through, 4 to 5 minutes. Add the tomato sauce and bring to a boil; reduce the heat to medium low and cook, uncovered, until the meat is cooked through, 2 to 3 minutes. Add the cooked rice and ¾ cup of the cheese, and stir until melted. Remove the skillet from the heat.
  6. Remove the peppers from the oven and spoon the meat filling evenly into the peppers. Sprinkle with the remaining ¾ cup cheese and place back in the oven. Roast for 10 to 15 minutes, until the filling is hot and the cheese is melted and bubbling, and serve.
